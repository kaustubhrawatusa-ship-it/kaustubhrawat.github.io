Chapter I

The Boy from
the Mountains

Every great story has an origin. For Kaustubh Rawat, it begins in the hill towns of Uttarakhand — where thin mountain air, discipline, and an early love for the game set the stage for everything that followed.

His first real test came at a state tournament, where as a young, unproven player he stepped onto the court against a nationally-ranked opponent. He won. That result didn't just earn a trophy — it announced that a serious player had arrived.

"He didn't have the facilities. He had the fire. And on that day, it turned out fire was enough."

What followed was a career built on relentless upward movement — through sub-junior and junior national circuits, into senior rankings, and eventually onto the international BWF stage. Each chapter earned, none of it given.

Chapter II

Athlete. Coach.
Builder.

Most athletes choose one lane. Kaustubh occupies three simultaneously. He is an active international competitor on the BWF circuit — still training, still entering draws, still chasing ranking points. At the same time, he coaches, and recently stepped into the role of Head of Business Development in the Delhi badminton ecosystem.

That rare combination — athlete, coach, and business developer all at once — is what defines where he is today. Not retired. Not transitioning. All of it, at the same time.
